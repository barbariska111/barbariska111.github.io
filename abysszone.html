The hadal zone refers to the ocean’s deepest region that is located within the oceanic trenches. This zone extends from 20,000 ft downwards to 36,000 ft and ranges between 3.7 to 6.8 miles. Extremely high hydrostatic pressures, complete lack of sunlight, scarcity of nutrients, and low temperatures characterize the deepest ocean trenches. Most organisms found in the hadal ecosystems are either scavengers or detrivores.
